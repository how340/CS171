Visualization Critique

This visualization is clearly targeted at the people interested in the compensation of tech companies, especially at Google. The graph attempts to display information on the compensation range of different positions at Google, and tries to answer the question that how well are these people at google paid. The data displayed here are interval (ordinal that could be have mathematical operations) data in the format of the pie chart.

For each specific group of employee, their data is projected onto the pie chart through different color codings and a slice of the pie. The larger the salary range is, the larger the area occupied on the chart is.

In my opinion, this visualization is not a good visualition. First, the graph is a bit busy visually. There are a lot of contrasting colors overlapping each other, and it can be difficult to discern different groups when they share a similar range. Second, the usage of pie chart here is not really appropriate. Pie chart should be used when everything on the chart can add to 1 or 100%. Here, the overall pie is used to convey a range of salary, which is not the best portrayal of information. Remaking this visualization as a boxplot can be very helpful here. Lastly, the human figures here don't really play a big role in this visualization. Replacing them with a straightforward color legend will be much cleaner.
